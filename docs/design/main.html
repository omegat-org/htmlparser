HTMLParser Documentation

Javadoc
You can browse the javadoc of all the classes from this link. The Javadoc of the classes, particularly HTMLParser, contains a lot of information that will help you understand how to use the parser. If you're the quintessential java programmer who just needs good javadoc to get started, this is the place to look. We recommend you focus initially on the org.htmlparser and org.htmlparser.tags packages.

Getting Started
There's no better way of getting started than to look at Sample programs made with the parser. You should find most of the programs that you'd ever want to make. Most of the Sample Programs have design commentaries, so you can also treat this as a tutorial on the parser and its design.

HTMLParser - Patterns Used
The design of HTML Parser has evolved over a period of time (over a year now) with input from various experts. A lot of refactorings have taken place, and a couple of patterns have come in which makes for interesting study. If you are the OO-nerd who loves to understand hierarchies and frameworks, you might find this interesting.

Test-driven Development
We'll take a look at the testing framework in place - this is a must-see for developers on the htmlparser project, in order to follow the XP paradigm of Test-Driven Development.
